# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We, the contributors and maintainers of **CalculusRuntime**, pledge to make participation in our project and community a welcoming, respectful, and harassment-free experience for everyone — regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We are committed to building an open, collaborative community where people of all backgrounds can contribute to open-source mathematics and machine learning tooling with confidence and dignity.

---

## Our Standards

The following behaviours contribute to a positive environment and are expected of all community members:

- **Be respectful and inclusive.** Use welcoming and inclusive language. Respect differing viewpoints and experiences. Remember that people contributing from different mathematical or software backgrounds may approach problems differently — that diversity is a strength.

- **Be constructive in feedback.** Critique ideas, not people. When reviewing code or documentation, focus on what can be improved and explain why. Unhelpful or dismissive feedback ("this is wrong", "just google it") is not acceptable.

- **Be collaborative.** Share knowledge openly. If you know why something works a certain way in SLaNg, the inference engine, or the frontend, explain it. Help newcomers get oriented.

- **Be patient.** Not everyone has the same experience with symbolic math, Python ML pipelines, or multi-submodule git workflows. Take time to help others understand rather than assuming knowledge.

- **Accept responsibility.** If you make a mistake — in code, in communication, or in your understanding of the project — acknowledge it, correct it, and move forward. We all make mistakes; what matters is how we handle them.

- **Prioritise the health of the project.** Make decisions that are good for the community and the long-term maintainability of the codebase, not just for your immediate goals.

---

## Unacceptable Behaviour

The following behaviours are unacceptable in any project space, whether in code comments, pull request reviews, issues, discussions, or any other forum:

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination in any form.
- Sexualised language, imagery, or unwelcome sexual attention or advances.
- Personal or political attacks — including comments about someone's background, identity, or affiliation.
- Trolling, deliberate disruption, or inflammatory provocation.
- Publishing private information about another person (doxxing) without explicit permission.
- Sustained disruption of discussions, code reviews, or community events.
- Dismissing contributors on the basis of experience level. Everyone was a beginner once.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces managed by QuantumLogicsLabs, including:

- The `CalculusRuntime` repository and all its submodules (`calculussolver`, `slang`, `frontend`, `backend`)
- GitHub Issues, Pull Requests, Discussions, and code review comments
- Any community channels (chat, forums, social media) operated under the project's name
- In-person or virtual events where someone is representing the project

It also applies when an individual is publicly representing the project or its community outside these spaces.

---

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behaviour may be reported by contacting the project maintainers at the email address listed in the repository's security/contact policy.

All reports will be handled with discretion and confidentiality. The maintainers are obligated to maintain the confidentiality of the reporter.

**Enforcement steps:**

1. **Warning.** A private written warning will be issued, providing clarity about the nature of the violation and why the behaviour was inappropriate.

2. **Temporary suspension.** A temporary ban from any interaction or public communication in the project spaces. Violating this ban may result in a permanent ban.

3. **Permanent ban.** A permanent ban from all public interaction within the project community.

Maintainers who do not follow or enforce this Code of Conduct in good faith may face temporary or permanent consequences as determined by other members of the project's leadership.

---

## Appeals

If you believe you have been unfairly sanctioned, you may appeal by contacting the project maintainers in writing. Appeals will be reviewed by a maintainer who was not involved in the original decision, where possible. The outcome of an appeal is final.
